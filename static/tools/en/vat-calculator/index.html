❓ FAQ

VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax applied at each stage of production and distribution. In the UK the standard rate is 20%, in the EU it varies from 15-27% by country. In the US, sales tax is used instead of VAT.
Divide the gross price by (1 + VAT rate). For example, with 20% VAT: £120 ÷ 1.20 = £100 net. The VAT amount is £120 - £100 = £20. Use the "Remove VAT" mode in this calculator.
Common standard rates: UK 20%, Germany 19%, France 20%, Italy 22%, Spain 21%, Korea 10%, Japan 10%, Australia 10% (GST). Reduced rates apply to certain goods — check your country's tax authority for specifics.
