? Frequently Asked Questions

Man-nai (©©│¬└╠) is the international standard age calculation: you start at 0 at birth and gain 1 year on each birthday. Since June 2023, this is Korea's official legal age system, replacing the traditional counting age.
Traditional Korean counting age (╝╝┤┬│¬└╠) starts at 1 at birth (you're already "1" when born) and increases by 1 every January 1st, regardless of actual birthday. International age (man-nai) increases on your actual birthday, making Korean counting age typically 1-2 years higher.
Effective June 28, 2023, this law standardized all administrative and legal documents in South Korea to use man-nai (international age calculation), ending the decades-long confusion between the three different age systems.
If your birthday hasn't passed yet this year, your man-nai = current year - birth year - 1. For example, someone born December 1990 has man-nai of 35 in July 2026 (2026 - 1990 - 1 = 35).
