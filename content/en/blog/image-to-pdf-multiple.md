## When You Need Multiple Images in One PDF

- Submitting scanned multi-page documents (passport + visa + tickets)
- Creating a photo portfolio for a client or employer
- Archiving a set of receipts for expense reporting
- Sending a series of screenshots as one document

## How Page Order Works

Images are placed into the PDF **in the order they appear in the list** after uploading. If you upload 5 images, image #1 becomes page 1, image #2 becomes page 2, and so on.

If you realize the order is wrong, remove individual images using the × button and re-upload them in the correct sequence.

## Upload Tips for Large Batches

When selecting multiple files from your file browser:
- **Windows**: Hold `Ctrl` and click each file, or `Shift`+click to select a range
- **Mac**: Hold `Cmd` and click each file
- **Mobile**: Your browser's file picker typically lets you select multiple images from your gallery

You can also drag multiple files simultaneously from your desktop directly into the upload zone.

## Mixed Orientation Handling

The **Auto** orientation setting is especially useful for multi-image PDFs where some images are portrait and others are landscape. Each page automatically rotates to best fit its image. The result is a clean PDF where every image fills as much of its page as possible without cropping.

## Output File Quality

The converter uses the original image data at full resolution. Images are scaled to fit within A4 dimensions (210×297mm) while preserving their aspect ratio. No upscaling occurs — so the quality of your output PDF is limited by your input images.

## Example: Expense Report PDF

Say you have 8 receipt photos from a business trip:
1. Upload all 8 JPG photos at once
2. Confirm order (oldest receipt first)
3. Set Orientation: Auto, Margin: 10mm
4. Convert → Download `converted.pdf`
5. Attach to your expense report

One clean, multi-page PDF instead of 8 separate image attachments.
