## What Does D-Day Mean?

D-Day (or Designated Day) originally came from military planning, but today it's used to mark any significant target date. It counts down how many days remain until an event — or counts up from a past milestone.

## D-Day Notation

- **D-100**: 100 days remaining until the target date
- **D-0**: Today is the target date
- **D+30**: 30 days have passed since the target date

## Effective D-Day Strategies

### Study Planning

Set the exam date as D-0. With D-100, you can plan exactly 100 units of content — one per day. Visibility of the countdown creates urgency and structure.

### Project Deadline Management

Use milestone markers: at D-30, D-14, D-7, and D-3, define exactly what must be complete. Working backwards from the deadline is far more effective than planning forward from "now."

### Relationship Milestones

Calculate the 100th, 500th, or 1000th day of a relationship, or track days until a shared anniversary. Planning ahead ensures you never miss an important moment.
