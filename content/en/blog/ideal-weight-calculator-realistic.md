## Why Most Diet Attempts Fail

Studies consistently show that roughly 80% of people who lose weight regain it within 5 years. The reasons are well-understood:

- Too restrictive, triggering binge cycles
- Focus on a number (scale weight) rather than habits
- No plan for the maintenance phase
- Unrealistic timeline expectations

The goal isn't to "lose weight" — it's to build the habits that naturally lead to a healthy weight and keep you there.

## Setting a Realistic Timeline

After calculating your ideal weight, figure out the gap from your current weight:

| Gap from Ideal | Realistic Timeline |
|---------------|-------------------|
| 5 kg | 2–3 months |
| 10 kg | 4–6 months |
| 20 kg | 9–14 months |
| 30+ kg | 18+ months |

These timelines assume a safe loss rate of 0.5–1 kg per week. Anything faster is rarely sustainable.

## Phase 1: Building the Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

Don't overhaul everything at once. Pick two habits to change:

1. **Eliminate liquid calories**: Swap soda, juice, and alcohol for water and unsweetened tea
2. **Add 20 minutes of walking daily**: Low barrier, immediate metabolic benefit

These two changes alone often result in 1–2 kg of weight loss in the first month without any other dietary changes.

## Phase 2: Structured Progress (Month 2–4)

Once baseline habits are locked in, layer on:

- **Portion awareness**: Use smaller plates, stop eating before feeling full
- **Protein at every meal**: Keeps you full longer and preserves muscle
- **Meal planning**: Reduces impulse eating and takeout
- **2–3 strength training sessions per week**: Builds muscle, boosts metabolism

## Phase 3: Breaking Through Plateaus

Weight loss always slows or stalls at some point. This is normal — your body adapts to a lower calorie intake. Options:

1. **Calorie cycling**: Alternate between a larger deficit (4 days) and maintenance (3 days)
2. **Change your workout**: New exercise type creates new metabolic demand
3. **Short diet break**: 1–2 weeks at maintenance calories can "reset" the metabolic rate

## The Maintenance Mindset

Reaching your ideal weight is easier than maintaining it. The habits that got you there need to continue — not with the same intensity, but consistently. Allow ±2–3 kg fluctuations as normal; only course-correct if the trend persists for more than 4 weeks.

## Getting Professional Support

If you have significant weight to lose (20+ kg), pre-existing conditions, or a history of disordered eating, work with:
- A registered dietitian for personalized nutrition planning
- A doctor for monitoring and medical support
- A certified personal trainer for safe, effective exercise programming
