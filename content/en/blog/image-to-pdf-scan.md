## Your Phone Is Already a Scanner

Modern smartphone cameras are capable of producing document scans that are indistinguishable from flatbed scanner output — if you know the right technique. Combine this with the Image to PDF Converter and you have a full document digitization workflow that fits in your pocket.

## How to Take a Clean Document Photo

### Lighting
- Natural light from a window is ideal
- Avoid direct sunlight (creates harsh shadows or glare)
- Turn off flash — it causes specular reflections on glossy paper
- Overcast daylight is perfect for even, shadow-free illumination

### Positioning
- Lay the document completely flat
- Shoot straight down (perpendicular to the document)
- Fill the frame — the document should take up ~90% of the image
- Keep all four corners visible; don't crop the edges

### Stability
- Rest your elbows on the table for stability
- Use your camera's self-timer to avoid camera shake
- On iOS, use the volume button to take the shot without touching the screen

### Background
- Place light documents on a dark surface for clear edge detection
- Place dark documents on a light surface

## Recommended Document Scanning Apps

Before converting to PDF, these free apps can automatically detect document edges and correct perspective distortion:

- **Microsoft Lens** (iOS/Android): Excellent automatic document detection, integrates with OneDrive
- **Adobe Scan** (iOS/Android): Includes OCR for searchable PDFs
- **Google PhotoScan**: Optimized for photos, reduces glare automatically

## Workflow on Mobile

1. Open the Image to PDF Converter in your mobile browser
2. Tap the upload zone
3. Select "Camera" to scan now, or "Photo Library" to use existing shots
4. Select multiple images at once if your browser supports it
5. Set orientation to Auto, margin to 10mm
6. Tap Convert — your PDF downloads to the Files app (iOS) or Downloads folder (Android)

## Practical Use Cases

- **Receipts**: Photograph and PDF receipts immediately after purchase for expense tracking
- **Legal documents**: Scan signed contracts before mailing originals
- **ID documents**: Keep PDF copies of passport, ID cards (store securely, offline)
- **Handwritten notes**: Convert whiteboard photos or notebook pages to archivable PDFs

## Conclusion

With a steady hand, good lighting, and the Image to PDF Converter, you can produce clean, professional-looking PDF documents from any smartphone. No dedicated scanner required.
