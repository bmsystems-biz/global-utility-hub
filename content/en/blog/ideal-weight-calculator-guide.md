## What Is Ideal Body Weight?

Ideal Body Weight (IBW) is an estimated target weight range considered healthy for a given height and gender. No single number applies to everyone — bone density, muscle mass, and body frame all influence what "healthy" looks like. Using multiple formulas and averaging results gives a more realistic target range.

## Formula 1: Broca's Formula

Developed by French surgeon Paul Broca in the 19th century. Simple and widely cited.

```
Male:   (Height in cm − 100) × 0.9
Female: (Height in cm − 100) × 0.85
```

**Example — Male, 175cm**: (175 − 100) × 0.9 = **67.5 kg**

**Strengths**: Easy to calculate mentally  
**Weaknesses**: Tends to underestimate for shorter people, overestimate for taller people

## Formula 2: Lorentz Formula

Refines the Broca formula with a correction factor based on height deviation from 150cm.

```
Male:   Height − 100 − (Height − 150) / 4
Female: Height − 100 − (Height − 150) / 2
```

**Example — Male, 175cm**: 175 − 100 − (175 − 150) / 4 = **68.75 kg**

**Strengths**: More accurate for average heights (155–175cm)  
**Weaknesses**: Less reliable at height extremes

## Formula 3: BMI-22 Standard Weight

The most clinically referenced modern formula, targeting BMI 22 — the midpoint of the Asian normal range (18.5–22.9).

```
Ideal Weight (kg) = 22 × Height (m)²
```

**Example — 175cm**: 22 × (1.75)² = **67.4 kg**

**Strengths**: Directly tied to evidence-based BMI research, most used in clinical settings  
**Weaknesses**: Does not account for muscle mass or body frame

## Which Formula Should You Use?

The average of all three formulas provides the best single target. For a male at 175cm:

| Formula | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Broca | 67.5 kg |
| Lorentz | 68.75 kg |
| BMI-22 | 67.4 kg |
| **Average** | **67.9 kg** |

Use this average as your target, but allow a ±5 kg range depending on your body type and composition.

## Beyond the Numbers

If you're heavily muscular (athlete, weightlifter), the BMI-based formula will understate a healthy weight for you. Similarly, if you have a naturally fine bone structure, even the lower end of these formulas may feel slightly high. Use the calculator as a guide, not a prescription.
