## Why Business Card QR Codes Work

A business card has limited space — name, title, phone, email, and maybe a website. A QR code punches through that constraint by linking to whatever you want: your full portfolio, LinkedIn profile, Linktree page, demo reel, or digital business card.

## What to Link From Your Business Card QR Code

### Option 1: Personal Link Hub
Create a Linktree or personal landing page with all your important links. One QR code links to everything — portfolio, social profiles, contact form, calendar booking.

### Option 2: vCard (Automatic Contact Import)
Encode a vCard so that scanning the QR code opens the "Add Contact" screen automatically. Format:
```
BEGIN:VCARD
VERSION:3.0
FN:Jane Smith
TEL:+15551234567
EMAIL:jane@example.com
ORG:Acme Corp
URL:https://janesmith.com
END:VCARD
```

### Option 3: LinkedIn Profile
Simply encode your LinkedIn profile URL. Many professionals prefer this for keeping things simple and professional.

### Option 4: Portfolio or Work Samples
For designers, developers, photographers, or writers — link directly to your portfolio site.

## Technical Requirements for Printing

**Minimum size**: Print at least 2cm × 2cm (about 0.8 inches square) for reliable scanning. On a standard business card, this typically means a QR code that takes up roughly 20–25% of one side.

**Generate at 512px**: Export your QR code at the highest resolution for crisp print quality. A 512×512px PNG at 300 DPI prints cleanly at up to 4.3cm × 4.3cm.

**Error correction level H**: Business cards get worn, bent, and sometimes wet. Level H allows 30% of the code to be damaged and still scan successfully.

**Quiet zone**: Leave at least 4 modules (the small squares) of white space around the entire QR code. Don't let other design elements crowd it.

## Testing Before You Print

Print one test copy and scan it with:
- iPhone camera (native iOS QR scanner)
- Android camera (Google Lens)
- A dedicated QR code scanning app

If all three read it correctly, you're ready to print the full batch.

## Design Tips

- **Dark on white**: Standard black QR on white background is the most reliable
- **Colored QR codes**: You can use brand colors but keep the contrast ratio high
- **Logo overlay**: Some QR generators allow a logo in the center — if you do this, use error correction H to compensate
- **Label it**: Add small text like "Scan to connect" or "Portfolio →" near the QR code so people know what they'll get
