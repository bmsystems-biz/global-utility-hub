## The Limitation of One-Size-Fits-All Formulas

A 175cm male who lifts weights 5 days a week might weigh 85 kg with 12% body fat — far above the 68 kg suggested by ideal weight formulas, yet in excellent health. Conversely, someone who weighs exactly what the formula suggests but has 35% body fat is technically at "ideal weight" but carries significant metabolic risk.

The formula is a useful starting point. Your actual target should incorporate more context.

## Body Type Considerations

### Ectomorph (Lean, Hard to Gain)
- Tend to be naturally lean with narrow frames
- Formula results may be slightly high — target the lower end of the formula range
- Focus more on strength/muscle building than on weight loss

### Mesomorph (Athletic, Responsive)
- Respond well to both training and diet changes
- Formula averages generally apply well
- Balance between fat reduction and muscle retention

### Endomorph (Rounder, Easy to Gain)
- Body fat percentage is a more useful metric than weight
- Target body fat % rather than a scale number
- The formula average is a reasonable target, but reaching it requires consistent diet discipline

## Better Metrics Than Scale Weight

### Body Fat Percentage
- Men: 10–20% is healthy
- Women: 18–28% is healthy
- Measured by DEXA scan (most accurate), hydrostatic weighing, or bioelectrical impedance scales

### Waist Circumference (Abdominal Obesity)
- Men: Under 90cm (Korean/Asian guideline) or 94cm (WHO)
- Women: Under 85cm (Korean/Asian) or 80cm (WHO)
- Abdominal fat is the most dangerous metabolically

### Waist-to-Height Ratio
- Under 0.5 is generally healthy across all populations
- Formula: Waist (cm) ÷ Height (cm)

## Calculating Your Personal Target Weight

If you know your body fat percentage:

```
Lean body mass = Current weight × (1 − Current body fat %)
Target weight = Lean body mass ÷ (1 − Target body fat %)
```

**Example**: 80 kg, 30% body fat, targeting 20% body fat
- Lean body mass = 80 × 0.70 = 56 kg
- Target weight = 56 ÷ 0.80 = **70 kg**

Compare this to formula results and choose the more conservative (lower-risk) target.

## Practical Goal-Setting Steps

1. **Calculate formula average** using the Ideal Weight Calculator
2. **Measure waist circumference** — if over guideline, prioritize abdominal fat loss
3. **Check body fat %** if possible (gym machines give a rough estimate)
4. **Set a 3-month target**: Aim for 5% of current body weight lost, regardless of formula result
5. **Reassess quarterly**: Adjust the goal based on health markers and how you feel

## Conclusion

Ideal weight formulas are a useful compass, not a GPS. Use them to understand the general direction, then personalize your target based on body composition, health markers, and what feels sustainable for your lifestyle.
